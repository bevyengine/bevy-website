A complete game can use all sorts of different files.
Most of these are tangible pieces of art that you may be familiar with (like sounds, sprites, models, or fonts), while others can be more abstract (like level layouts, enemy statistics, or even scripts).
In game development all of these items are known as **assets**, and if we want to use them in our games, we first have to load them.

This chapter will cover the underlying machinery of using and loading assets in Bevy, helping you structure your game to work with them effectively.
